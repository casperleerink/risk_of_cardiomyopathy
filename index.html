Risk calculator for cardiomyopathy in childhood cancer survivors

This calculator can be used in childhood cancer survivors to estimate the probability of cardiomyopathy within 10 years from the surveillance echocardiogram at >5 years after cancer diagnosis. Cardiomyopathy was defined as a left ventricular ejection fraction <40% during follow-up, the treshold for pharmacological heart failure treatment initiation according to the European Society of Cardiology heart failure guideline. The risk calculator was developed for adult >5-year childhood cancer survivors treated with anthracyclines and/or chest-directed radiotherapy without heart failure or a left ventricular ejection fraction <40% at time of the risk assessment. The prediction model to estimate the risk was developed and validated in the publication from Leerink et al. 2020 (REF..).
